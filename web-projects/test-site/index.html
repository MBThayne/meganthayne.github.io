Restoring Habitat With Beaver Dams

Land use changes and development have damaged the habitat for many species in Oregon. Riparian restoration projects have sought to address the negative effects of human activity. New research shows the benefits of beaver activity on the landscape. Beaver dams has shown to enhance watershed conditions by raising the water table in the valley floor leading to more:

  • forage
  • diversidified and improved wildlife and fisheries habitat
  • improved flood control

Read "Beaver To Restore Salmon Habitat" to learn more about how beavers can be a tool in restoration projects.
